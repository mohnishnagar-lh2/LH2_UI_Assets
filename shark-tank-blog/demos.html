Demos

Shark Tank Blog subscription flows.

Five live walkthroughs of different ways to take a reader from "I want to support this" to "I'm an active subscriber." Each is a fully clickable demo showing the page-by-page flow.

Flow demos — different orderings of landing / login / payment
Flow A
Login first, then pay.
CTA→ landing→ login→ payment
User authenticates before paying. After Stripe, they're already logged in — no email round-trip needed. Highest-friction but cleanest account state.
Architecture
Flow B
Pay first, login via email link.
CTA→ landing→ payment→ email link
User pays without an account. After payment, a magic-link email activates ad-free on click. Lowest checkout friction, requires inbox round-trip.
Architecture
Flow C
Direct pay, no landing.
CTA→ payment + messaging→ email link
CTA goes straight to the payment page; the value proposition lives on the left of the checkout itself. Shortest funnel; magic-link activates after pay.
Architecture
Flow D
Direct payment popup.
CTA→ modal opens→ pay inline→ email link
CTA opens a Stripe-style payment modal in place — no route change. After payment, the modal swaps to a "check your email" success state. Email link activates.
Architecture
Flow E
Sales-oriented checkout.
CTA→ sales checkout→ email link
Payment page with a full sales pitch on the left — urgency banner, hero image, comparison table, testimonial, money-back guarantee. Designed for cold-traffic conversion.
Architecture
Flow F
Guest checkout — no account needed.
CTA→ guest pay→ confirm email→ activate
Pay-first flow with explicit "Checkout as a guest" framing. No password, no account form. Email confirmation step takes center stage on the success page.
Architecture
Flow G
A/B test the CTA messaging.
"Go Ad-Free"vs "Become an Insider"
Same homepage, two different CTA copy treatments — control vs early-access framing. Includes mock conversion metrics (CTR, conversions) for each variant.
